# Maharashtra RC Transfer — Complete Branching Checklist

> Second-hand car purchase / sale in Maharashtra  
> Based on: Motor Vehicles Act 1988 (§50, §51), Central MV Rules 1989 (Rule 81), Maharashtra MV Rules 1989  
> Last updated reference: May 2026

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

- Each item has a **✅ YES** path and a **❌ NO** path.
- If **YES** → proceed to the next item.
- If **NO** → follow the **→ FIX** block under that item before proceeding.
- Items marked **`[N/A if no loan]`**, **`[N/A if same RTO]`** etc. can be skipped when the condition does not apply to you.
- Items marked **🔴 CRITICAL** must not be skipped under any circumstance.
- Items marked **🟡 IMPORTANT** can cause delays if missed.
- Items marked **🔵 ADVISORY** are strongly recommended but not legally mandatory.

# PART 1 — BUYER CHECKLIST

---

## PHASE 1 — PRE-PURCHASE VERIFICATION

> Complete this phase **before paying any money or signing anything**

---

### B-01 🔴 CRITICAL — Vehicle checked on VAHAN portal

**What to check:**
Visit `vahan.parivahan.gov.in` → _Know Your Vehicle_ → Enter registration number.

Verify all of the following fields:

- Registered owner name
- Registration validity / expiry date
- Hypothecation status (bank name, if any)
- Insurance validity
- PUC certificate validity
- Pending challans / dues
- Theft / blacklist status
- Fitness certificate (for older vehicles)

**✅ YES** → All fields verified and clean. Proceed to B-02.

**❌ NO / Not done yet**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Go to `vahan.parivahan.gov.in` or download the **mParivahan** app.
> 2. Enter the vehicle registration number and captcha.
> 3. Screenshot or note down every field shown.
> 4. Cross-check each field against the physical RC book.
> 5. **Do NOT proceed with the purchase if you find:**
>    - Active hypothecation (bank name shown)
>    - Pending challans (unless negotiated and cleared)
>    - Theft flag / blacklist status
>    - Expired registration
> 6. Only after all fields are clean should you move forward.

---

### B-02 🔴 CRITICAL — Owner name on RC matches seller's identity proof

**What to check:**

- Name on RC book / smart card must **exactly match** the seller's Aadhaar / PAN / Passport.
- Even minor spelling differences are a red flag.

**✅ YES** → Names match exactly. Proceed to B-03.

**❌ NO — Name mismatch found**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Ask the seller to produce all legal documents explaining the discrepancy:
>    - Marriage certificate (if name changed after marriage)
>    - Affidavit for name change
>    - Court order for name change
>    - Gazette notification of name change
> 2. If no valid legal explanation exists → **stop the transaction immediately.**
> 3. A mismatch with no explanation could indicate a stolen vehicle, a fraudulently transferred RC, or an impersonation attempt.
> 4. Contact the local RTO directly to verify the registered owner's identity using the registration number.
> 5. File a police complaint if fraud is suspected.

---

### B-03 🔴 CRITICAL — Chassis number and engine number physically verified

**What to check:**

- Locate the **chassis number** stamped on the vehicle body (usually on the firewall under the bonnet, or on the door pillar / floor).
- Locate the **engine number** stamped on the engine block.
- Match **character-by-character** with the RC book / smart card.
- Also match with the VAHAN portal record.

**✅ YES** → Both numbers match RC and VAHAN exactly. Proceed to B-04.

**❌ NO — Number mismatch found**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. **Do not buy this vehicle under any circumstances until fully resolved.**
> 2. A mismatch indicates one or more of the following:
>    - The RC belongs to a different vehicle
>    - The chassis / engine has been replaced without RTO endorsement
>    - The vehicle has been tampered with (possible stolen vehicle with swapped RC)
> 3. Ask the seller to produce an RTO endorsement or court order permitting engine/chassis replacement.
> 4. If no valid documentation exists → abandon the purchase.
> 5. If you suspect fraud, file a complaint at the nearest police station and inform the RTO.

---

### B-04 🔴 CRITICAL — No active hypothecation (vehicle is loan-free)

**What to check:**

- VAHAN portal → `Hypothecation By` field must be blank / NA.
- Physical RC book → no bank name endorsed / stamped.
- **Note:** Some older RTOs do not update VAHAN in real time — if RC shows a bank name but VAHAN does not, treat it as **active hypothecation**.

**✅ YES** → No hypothecation found on both VAHAN and RC. Proceed to B-05.

**❌ NO — Active loan / hypothecation found**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. **Do NOT pay for the vehicle until hypothecation is fully cleared and RC is updated.**
> 2. The seller must take all of the following steps first:
>    - Repay the entire outstanding car loan (check for prepayment penalty — up to 2% at some lenders).
>    - Obtain a **Loan Closure Certificate** (nil balance letter) from the bank.
>    - Obtain the **Original Bank NOC** — must be a hard copy with original ink signature and rubber stamp. Soft copy / printout is NOT accepted by the RTO.
>    - Obtain **Form 35** signed by both the bank's authorised signatory and the vehicle owner (2 sets).
>    - File **HP Termination** at the **parent / original RTO** (the RTO where the vehicle is registered) with all required documents.
>    - Wait 7–30 working days for the RC to be updated.
>    - Confirm on VAHAN portal that `Hypothecation By` field is now blank / NA.
> 3. **Only after VAHAN confirms removal** should you proceed with payment.
> 4. Bank NOC is valid for **90 days** from date of issue — the seller must complete HP termination within this window.
> 5. See **Part 3 — Loan / Hypothecation Checklist** for the full detailed process.

---

### B-05 🟡 IMPORTANT — No pending traffic challans on the vehicle

**What to check:**

- `parivahan.gov.in` → _e-Challan_ → Check challan status using registration number.
- Also check on the VAHAN portal.

**✅ YES** → No pending challans. Proceed to B-06.

**❌ NO — Pending challans found**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. The seller must clear **all pending challans** — the RTO will not approve the RC transfer if any dues are outstanding.
> 2. Options:
>    - Seller clears all challans before the sale is finalized.
>    - OR: Deduct the challan amount from the agreed purchase price AND ensure the challans are cleared before submitting the transfer application.
> 3. After payment, verify clearance on the e-challan portal (takes 24–48 hours to reflect).
> 4. Obtain a **Challan Clearance Certificate** from the RTO as proof.
> 5. Submit this certificate along with the transfer application.

---

### B-06 🟡 IMPORTANT — No pending road tax dues

**What to check:**

- Ask the seller for road tax payment receipts.
- Cross-check on the VAHAN portal or contact the RTO directly.

**✅ YES** → Road tax is clear. Proceed to B-07.

**❌ NO — Road tax dues outstanding**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Seller is responsible for clearing all road tax dues before the sale.
> 2. Outstanding road tax will block the RC transfer at the RTO.
> 3. Seller must visit the RTO or pay through the state's designated tax payment portal.
> 4. Obtain a **Road Tax Clearance Certificate** from the RTO.
> 5. Submit this certificate along with the transfer application.

---

### B-07 🔴 CRITICAL — Vehicle is NOT reported stolen / blacklisted

**What to check:**

- VAHAN portal → theft / blacklist status field.
- NCRB (National Crime Records Bureau) check (especially important for out-of-state vehicles).

**✅ YES** → Vehicle is clean. Proceed to B-08.

**❌ NO — Vehicle flagged as stolen or blacklisted**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. **Do NOT buy this vehicle under any circumstances.**
> 2. Disengage from the seller immediately.
> 3. File a complaint at the nearest police station with all details of the seller and the vehicle.
> 4. Inform the local RTO about the attempted fraudulent sale.
> 5. If you have already made any payment, file a police FIR immediately and consult a lawyer.

---

### B-08 🟡 IMPORTANT — Insurance policy is valid (not expired)

**What to check:**

- Verify insurance expiry date on the physical insurance document, RC, and VAHAN portal.
- At minimum, **third-party insurance** must be active — it is mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act 1988.

**✅ YES** → Insurance is valid. Proceed to B-09.  
**`[N/A]`** → Insurance is expiring soon (within 30 days) — plan for renewal.

**❌ NO — Insurance expired**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Ask the seller to renew the insurance before handover — the vehicle must be insured for test drives and delivery.
> 2. Alternatively, you can purchase a new insurance policy in your name immediately after taking possession — but ensure there is no gap in coverage.
> 3. Negotiate the cost of a new policy into the sale price if the existing policy has lapsed.
> 4. After RC transfer is complete, update the insurance to reflect the new owner's name.

---

### B-09 🟡 IMPORTANT — PUC (Pollution Under Control) Certificate is valid

**What to check:**

- Check the validity date printed on the PUC certificate.
- Also verifiable on the VAHAN portal.
- **Valid periods:** Petrol/CNG — 6 months | Diesel — 3 months.

**✅ YES** → PUC is valid. Proceed to B-10.

**❌ NO — PUC expired or not available**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. PUC is **mandatory** for RC transfer — the RTO will reject the application without a valid PUC.
> 2. Visit any authorised PUC test centre (available at most petrol pumps and authorised testing stations).
> 3. Test takes 10–15 minutes. Cost: ₹60–₹100.
> 4. Fresh PUC certificate is issued on the spot.
> 5. Ensure this is done **before** submitting the RC transfer application to the RTO.

---

### B-10 🔵 BRANCHING — Vehicle is registered in Maharashtra (MH number plate)

**What to check:**

- First two characters of the registration number: `MH` = Maharashtra.
- If not MH → the **Out-of-State Car** process applies (see Part 4).

**✅ YES** → MH registration. Continue with this checklist.

**❌ NO — Non-MH registration (out-of-state vehicle)**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Switch to **Part 4 — Out-of-State Car Checklist** for the complete separate process.
> 2. Key additional steps involved:
>    - NOC from original state's RTO (Form 28 + Form 29 at that RTO)
>    - NCRB Clearance Certificate
>    - Road tax payment in Maharashtra (calculated on original invoice price, paid by Demand Draft)
>    - Physical vehicle inspection by Maharashtra RTO officer
>    - Re-registration with a new MH number plate
> 3. Timeline is significantly longer — allow **30+ days** minimum.
> 4. Out-of-state vehicle with active hypothecation → HP termination must be done at the original state's RTO first (not Maharashtra).

## PHASE 2 — DOCUMENTATION BEFORE DEAL

> Complete this phase **before signing the agreement or making final payment**

---

### B-11 🔴 CRITICAL — Original RC book is physically in hand

**What to check:**

- Must be the **physical original RC** book or smart card.
- **DigiLocker RC is explicitly NOT accepted** by Maharashtra RTO for ownership transfer.
- A photocopy is also not acceptable.

**✅ YES** → Original RC in hand. Proceed to B-12.

**❌ NO — Original RC is missing / lost / only DigiLocker available**

> **→ FIX — Seller must apply for Duplicate RC:**
>
> 1. **File an FIR** at the local police station having jurisdiction over the area where the RC was lost/stolen. Obtain the FIR copy.
> 2. **Inform the original RTO** in writing (formal letter) about the missing RC before applying for a duplicate.
> 3. **Apply for Duplicate RC** at the original RTO:
>    - Submit: FIR copy, application letter, Form 28 (Duplicate RC application), identity proof, address proof.
>    - Pay **50% of the original registration fee** applicable to the vehicle class.
> 4. RTO issues Duplicate RC after document verification — takes a few weeks.
> 5. **Do NOT accept a DigiLocker RC or printout as a substitute** — the RTO will not process the transfer.
> 6. Only proceed with the sale once the seller has the valid original or duplicate RC in hand.

---

### B-12 🔴 CRITICAL — Notarised sale agreement executed

**What to check:**
The agreement must be on stamp paper (applicable denomination per Maharashtra Stamp Act) and notarised by a Notary Public, and must include:

| Field               | Required Content                           |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Vehicle details     | Make, model, year, colour                  |
| Registration number | Full registration number                   |
| Chassis number      | Full chassis number                        |
| Engine number       | Full engine number                         |
| Sale price          | Exact amount in figures and words          |
| Payment mode        | Cash / NEFT / RTGS / cheque — with details |
| Vehicle condition   | As-is description; any damage disclosed    |
| Insurance details   | Policy number, insurer, expiry date        |
| RC status           | Original RC delivered; no hypothecation    |
| Handover date       | Exact date of physical handover            |
| Signatures          | Both seller and buyer, in front of notary  |

**Rule:** Seller signs **only after receiving full payment.**

**✅ YES** → Notarised agreement signed. Proceed to B-13.

**❌ NO — No notarised agreement**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Visit a Notary Public (available near most RTO offices and legal offices) with the seller.
> 2. Bring: original identity proofs of both parties, all vehicle details.
> 3. Get the agreement drafted (notary can help), signed by both parties in front of the notary, and notarised/stamped.
> 4. **Do not pay any significant amount** without a signed, notarised agreement.
> 5. This document is your primary legal protection in case of any post-sale dispute.

---

### B-13 🔴 CRITICAL — Form 29 received from seller (2 copies, both signed)

**What to check:**

- Form 29 = _Notice of Transfer of Ownership of Motor Vehicle_
- Must be signed by **both the buyer and the seller**.
- **2 copies** required:
  - Seller submits **1 copy** to the original RTO within 14 days of sale.
  - Buyer keeps **1 copy** — needed when applying at their local RTO.

**✅ YES** → Form 29 in hand, signed by both parties. Proceed to B-14.

**❌ NO — Form 29 not provided or not signed**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Download Form 29 from `parivahan.gov.in` → Forms → Form 29.
> 2. Or collect a physical copy from any local RTO office.
> 3. Fill in all fields: vehicle registration details, seller's details, buyer's details, date of transfer.
> 4. Both the seller and buyer must sign both copies.
> 5. Seller keeps their copy to submit to the RTO; buyer keeps the second copy.
> 6. Seller must submit their copy to the original RTO within **14 days of sale** (legal deadline under Section 50(a)(i)).

---

### B-14 🔵 ADVISORY — All vehicle support documents received from seller

**What to check:**

- Owner's manual
- Service history book (full maintenance records)
- All warranty documents (if still active)
- Original purchase invoice of the vehicle

**✅ YES** → All documents received. Proceed to Phase 3.

**`[N/A]`** → Some documents genuinely lost/unavailable — documented in sale agreement.

**❌ NO — Documents missing**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. While not mandatory for the RTO transfer, these documents significantly impact future **resale value** and **maintenance**.
> 2. Specifically insist on:
>    - **Service history book** — proves regular maintenance; very important for buyer's reference.
>    - **Original purchase invoice** — required for road tax calculation if the car is later moved to another state.
>    - **Warranty documents** — if any active warranty (manufacturer or extended) is still running.
> 3. Request service records from the authorised service centre using the chassis number if the physical book is lost.
> 4. Document which papers were and were not provided in the sale agreement itself.

## PHASE 3 — FILING RC TRANSFER AT RTO

> Complete within **30 days of purchase** (Section 50(b), MV Act 1988)

---

### B-15 🟡 IMPORTANT — Applied for Clearance Certificate (CC) from original RTO

`[N/A if vehicle is from the same RTO area as buyer]`

**What to check:**

- Required when the vehicle is registered in a **different RTO area** (even within Maharashtra).
- Confirms: no dues, no active cases, no encumbrances against the vehicle.
- CC is issued by the original (seller's) RTO and sent to the buyer.

**✅ YES** → CC applied for / received. Proceed to B-16.

**`[N/A]`** → Vehicle is from the same RTO area. Proceed to B-16.

**❌ NO — CC not yet applied for**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Submit the following to the **original RTO** (where the car is registered):
>    - Written request letter for Clearance Certificate
>    - Original RC
>    - Copy of valid insurance
>    - Copy of valid PUC
>    - Self-addressed envelope with **₹30 postage stamps** affixed (RTO uses this to mail the CC to you — for offline applications only)
> 2. This step is **free of charge** — no fee.
> 3. CC is typically issued within **30 days**.
> 4. You **cannot apply for RC transfer** at your local RTO until you have received this CC (for different-RTO vehicles).
> 5. Apply for CC as soon as possible after purchase — it runs in parallel with the 30-day transfer deadline.

---

### B-16 🔴 CRITICAL — Form 30 filled and ready for submission

**What to check:**

- Form 30 = _Application for Intimation and Transfer of Ownership_
- 1 copy, filled and signed by the **buyer**.
- Contains: vehicle details, new owner (buyer) name, address, insurance details, PUC details.

**✅ YES** → Form 30 filled and signed. Proceed to B-17.

**❌ NO — Form 30 not prepared**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Download Form 30 from `parivahan.gov.in` → Forms → Form 30.
> 2. Or collect a physical copy from the local RTO.
> 3. Fill in all fields carefully — ensure vehicle details match the RC exactly.
> 4. For **online application** on Parivahan portal:
>    - Log in to `vahan.parivahan.gov.in`
>    - Enter the application number generated when the seller filed Form 29
>    - Verify all pre-filled details
>    - Enter new owner info, insurance details, address details
>    - Proceed to payment
> 5. Submit Form 30 to your **local RTO** (the RTO in whose jurisdiction you live / where the vehicle will be kept).

---

### B-17 🟡 IMPORTANT — Photo with right-hand thumb impression obtained (certified)

**What to check:**

- A passport-size photograph with your **right-hand thumb impression in ink**, certified / attested by either:
  - A Notary Public, OR
  - A Gazetted Officer (Central/State Government Class I or Class II employee)

**✅ YES** → Certified photo ready. Proceed to B-18.

**❌ NO — Not done**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Get passport-size photos printed.
> 2. Press your right-hand thumb in ink on the photo.
> 3. Visit a Notary Public (near RTO offices, legal offices, court areas) or a Gazetted Officer.
> 4. They sign and stamp the photo, certifying it.
> 5. Cost: ₹50–₹100 at a notary. Takes 10–20 minutes.

---

### B-18 🔴 CRITICAL — All fees paid at RTO; receipts saved

**Fee schedule (Rule 81, Central MV Rules 1989 — Maharashtra):**

| Fee item                             | Amount          |
| ------------------------------------ | --------------- |
| RC Transfer fee — two-wheeler        | ₹150            |
| RC Transfer fee — four-wheeler (car) | ₹300            |
| Smart card fee (RC reissuance)       | ₹200 – ₹300     |
| Total estimated — two-wheeler        | ₹500 – ₹900     |
| Total estimated — four-wheeler       | ₹1,000 – ₹1,800 |
| NOC application (Form 28) fee        | ₹100            |
| Late filing penalty (after 30 days)  | ₹500            |

**✅ YES** → Fees paid; payment receipts saved. Proceed to B-19.

**❌ NO — Fees not paid**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Pay at the **RTO counter** (cash/DD) or **online** via Parivahan portal (UPI / net banking / debit card / credit card).
> 2. Always save / print **all payment receipts** — these are required for tracking the application status and as proof of submission.

---

### B-19 🔴 CRITICAL — Application filed within 30 days of purchase

**What to check:**

- Count from the **date of purchase / handover** — not from the date of the agreement or payment.
- Deadline: **30 days** under Section 50(b) of the Motor Vehicles Act 1988.
- Late fee: **₹500** after 30 days (Maharashtra schedule).

**✅ YES** → Filed within 30 days. Proceed to B-20.

**❌ NO — 30-day deadline missed**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. **Still file as soon as possible** — delayed filing is far better than not filing at all.
> 2. Be prepared to pay the **₹500 late fee** at the RTO counter.
> 3. Additional penalty under Section 177 of the MV Act is also possible (₹500–₹1,000).
> 4. The longer you wait, the higher the risk of legal complications:
>    - Challans issued to the vehicle remain in the seller's name, creating disputes.
>    - If the vehicle is involved in any accident or incident, legal liability questions become complex.
> 5. Bring all documents and explain the delay to the RTO officer — apply immediately.

---

### B-20 🟡 IMPORTANT — New RC received with buyer's name; all details verified

**What to check:**
After RTO processing, the new RC smart card / book must show:

- Buyer's correct name
- Buyer's correct address
- Vehicle details matching the car
- **No hypothecation entry** (must be blank)
- Correct engine number and chassis number

**✅ YES** → New RC received and verified. Proceed to B-21.

**❌ NO — RC not yet received / details incorrect**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. **Processing timelines:**
>    - Online application: 7–10 working days
>    - Offline application: up to 30 days
>    - Interstate transfer: 30+ days
> 2. Track application status on Parivahan portal using registration number and application number.
> 3. If delay is excessive (beyond 30 working days): visit the RTO with your payment receipt and application number and follow up in person.
> 4. If details on RC are **incorrect**: submit a correction application at the RTO immediately with the correct documents as evidence.

---

### B-21 🟡 IMPORTANT — Insurance policy transferred to buyer's name (or new policy purchased)

**What to check:**

- After RC is in buyer's name, the insurance policy must also reflect the new owner.
- Driving with insurance in the seller's name is a legal grey area and can complicate claims.

**✅ YES** → Insurance updated to buyer's name. ✅ Buyer checklist complete.

**❌ NO — Insurance not yet transferred**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Contact the insurer immediately after receiving the new RC.
> 2. Submit to insurer: new RC (in buyer's name), copy of sale agreement, Form 29, and buyer's identity proof.
> 3. Insurer endorses the policy in the buyer's name.
> 4. **Alternatively:** Cancel the existing policy and purchase a fresh policy in the buyer's name — ensure there is **no gap** in coverage.
> 5. **NCB (No Claim Bonus):** NCB is based on the policyholder's claim history — not on vehicle ownership. Confirm the NCB treatment with the insurer at the time of transfer.
> 6. If vehicle had hypothecation that has now been cleared: also inform insurer to remove the bank as co-owner from the policy.

# PART 2 — SELLER CHECKLIST

---

## PHASE 1 — PRE-SALE PREPARATION

---

### S-01 🔴 CRITICAL — All pending traffic challans cleared

**What to check:**

- `parivahan.gov.in` → _e-Challan_ → Enter registration number.
- Any outstanding challans will remain against your name post-sale until RC is transferred.

**✅ YES** → No pending challans. Proceed to S-02.

**❌ NO — Pending challans found**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Pay all dues online at `parivahan.gov.in/echallan` or at the issuing RTO / designated bank counters.
> 2. Payment modes accepted: net banking, UPI, debit/credit card.
> 3. Download and keep **all payment receipts** as proof.
> 4. Clearance reflects on the portal within 24–48 hours after payment.
> 5. Obtain a **Challan Clearance Certificate** from the RTO if the buyer or RTO requires formal proof.

---

### S-02 🔴 CRITICAL — All road tax dues cleared

**What to check:**

- No outstanding road tax against the vehicle.
- Check at the local RTO or on VAHAN portal.

**✅ YES** → Road tax is clear. Proceed to S-03.

**❌ NO — Road tax dues outstanding**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Visit the RTO or check the exact amount on the VAHAN portal / state transport portal.
> 2. Pay at the RTO or through Maharashtra state's designated online payment system.
> 3. Obtain a **Road Tax Clearance Certificate** as proof.
> 4. Without this, the RTO will block the transfer.

---

### S-03 🔴 CRITICAL — Car loan / EMI fully repaid

`[N/A if vehicle was never financed]`

**What to check:**

- If the vehicle was purchased with a loan, it must be **fully repaid** before selling.
- You cannot legally transfer ownership of a hypothecated vehicle without clearing the loan and terminating the HP at the RTO.

**✅ YES** → Loan fully repaid. Proceed to S-04.

**`[N/A]`** → Vehicle was never financed. Skip to S-05.

**❌ NO — Active EMI / loan outstanding**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Contact your bank / NBFC and request the **exact payoff / foreclosure amount** (outstanding principal + any prepayment penalty + accrued interest up to the payment date).
> 2. Check for **prepayment charges** — some lenders charge up to **2%** of outstanding principal for early closure.
> 3. Repay the full amount.
> 4. After payment, obtain a **Loan Closure Certificate** / No Dues Letter from the bank (official letterhead, nil balance stated).
> 5. This certificate is your proof of entitlement to the Bank NOC and Form 35.
> 6. Proceed to S-04.

---

### S-04 🔴 CRITICAL — Hypothecation (bank lien) removed from RC

`[N/A if vehicle was never financed]`

**What to check:**

- The RC must be officially updated at the RTO to remove the bank's name.
- **Bank NOC alone is NOT sufficient** — HP termination must be filed and completed at the RTO.
- Confirm on VAHAN portal: `Hypothecation By` = blank / NA.

**✅ YES** → HP terminated; RC updated; VAHAN confirms no hypothecation. Proceed to S-05.

**`[N/A]`** → Vehicle was never financed. Skip to S-05.

**❌ NO — Hypothecation still active**

> **→ FIX:**
> See full details in **Part 3 — Loan / Hypothecation Checklist**.  
> Summary steps:
>
> 1. Obtain Original Bank NOC (hard copy, signed & stamped) — valid for 90 days.
> 2. Obtain Form 35 (signed by both bank's authorised signatory + you) — 2 sets.
> 3. Submit Form 35 + Bank NOC + all required documents to the **parent / original RTO** (the RTO where the vehicle is registered — HP termination CANNOT be done at a different RTO).
> 4. Wait 7–30 working days for the RC to be updated.
> 5. Verify removal on VAHAN portal.
> 6. Inform your insurer to remove the bank as co-owner from the insurance policy.

---

### S-05 🔴 CRITICAL — Original RC book is in your possession and valid

**What to check:**

- Physical original RC book or smart card — not DigiLocker, not photocopy.
- If lost: Duplicate RC must be obtained before sale.

**✅ YES** → Original RC available. Proceed to S-06.

**❌ NO — Original RC is missing**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. **File an FIR** at the local police station (jurisdiction of where RC was lost/stolen). Obtain FIR copy.
> 2. **Write a formal letter** to the original RTO informing them that the RC has been lost/stolen.
> 3. **Apply for Duplicate RC** at the original RTO:
>    - Submit: FIR copy, letter, Form 28 (Duplicate RC application), identity proof, address proof.
>    - Pay **50% of the original registration fee** (vehicle class dependent).

**✅ YES** → Insurance valid. Proceed to Phase 2.

**`[N/A]`** → Expiring within 30 days — buyer should be informed.

### S-12 🔴 CRITICAL — Forms 29 + 30 submitted to original RTO within 14 days

| Vehicle registration           | Deadline                      |
| ------------------------------ | ----------------------------- |

| Registered outside Maharashtra | **45 days** from date of sale |

>    - RTO procedural fine: up to ₹100
>    - Section 177 MV Act penalty: ₹500–₹1,000
> 4. **Until you have an RTO acknowledgement / receipt**, you remain legally liable for any challans, accidents, or crimes involving that vehicle.
> 5. **Alternative — RPAD post:** If you cannot visit in person, send Forms 29 and 30 via **Registered Post with Acknowledgement Due (RPAD)** to the original RTO. The postal acknowledgement is accepted by law as equivalent to in-person submission under Section 50(a)(i)(B).

> 3. **Why this is critical:**
>    - This receipt is your **legal protection from all post-sale liability**.
>    - Without it, if the buyer gets challans, causes an accident, or commits a crime using the car, you remain legally exposed as the registered owner.
>    - Once you have this acknowledgement, you are legally indemnified of all traffic, tax, criminal, and legal liabilities pertaining to the vehicle.
> 4. **Keep this document permanently** — even after full RC transfer is confirmed.

## STEP A — CONFIRM LOAN STATUS

---

### L-01 🔴 CRITICAL — Hypothecation status confirmed on VAHAN portal

**What to check:**

- `vahan.parivahan.gov.in` → _Know Your Vehicle_ → Enter registration number.
- Field: `Hypothecation By` — if a bank/NBFC name appears, hypothecation is active.
- Also physically check RC book for bank name endorsement.

**⚠️ Important note:** Some older RTOs do not update VAHAN immediately — if the RC shows a bank name but VAHAN does not, treat it as **active hypothecation** until confirmed otherwise.

**✅ YES** → Hypothecation status confirmed (active or not). Proceed to L-02.

**❌ NO — Not checked yet**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Go to `vahan.parivahan.gov.in` → _Know Your Vehicle_.
> 2. Enter registration number.
> 3. Note the `Hypothecation By` field.
> 4. If blank/NA → no hypothecation. Proceed.
> 5. If a bank name appears → see L-02 onwards for the full clearance process.

---

### L-02 🔴 CRITICAL — (Seller) Loan fully repaid; nil balance confirmed

`[N/A if no loan existed]`

**What to check:**

- All EMI payments completed **or** full foreclosure payment made.
- Bank / NBFC has issued a **Loan Closure Certificate** confirming nil outstanding balance.

**✅ YES** → Loan closed; closure certificate obtained. Proceed to L-03.

**❌ NO — Loan still outstanding**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Contact your bank / NBFC customer care or branch and request the exact **payoff amount** (also called foreclosure amount):
>    - Outstanding principal
>    - Any prepayment / foreclosure penalty (up to 2% at some lenders — confirm upfront)
>    - Accrued interest up to the payment date
> 2. Make the full payment.
> 3. After payment, obtain the **Loan Closure Certificate** (No Dues Letter) — must be on official bank letterhead confirming nil balance.
> 4. This certificate is your proof of entitlement to the Bank NOC.

---

### L-03 🔴 CRITICAL — Original Bank NOC obtained (hard copy only)

**What to check:**

- Bank's **original hard-copy NOC** — with the bank official's original ink signature and official rubber stamp.
- **Soft copy, scanned printout, PDF printout = NOT accepted by any RTO.**
- NOC must state: "Loan has been fully repaid. Bank has no objection to removal of hypothecation from the vehicle's RC."
- **NOC is valid for 90 days from date of issue.**

**✅ YES** → Original hard-copy NOC obtained. Note the expiry date (90 days from issue). Proceed to L-04.

**❌ NO — NOC not obtained or in wrong format**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Request the NOC from your bank branch after loan closure — in writing.
> 2. Insist on the **original hard copy** with ink signature and rubber stamp.
> 3. **If bank branch has closed or relocated:**
>    - Call the bank's customer care helpline — they redirect to the new branch or process through the regional office.
>    - Some banks allow **online NOC requests** via their website or netbanking (e.g., HDFC Bank — via NetBanking for eligible accounts).
>    - Raise a formal written complaint to the bank's Nodal Officer / Regional Head if unresponsive.
>    - After full repayment, the bank is legally obligated to issue the NOC.
> 4. **Track the 90-day validity window** from the issue date — if it expires, request a fresh NOC.

---

### L-04 🔴 CRITICAL — Form 35 obtained (signed by both bank and vehicle owner)

**What to check:**

- Form 35 = _Notice for Termination of Hire-Purchase / Lease / Hypothecation Agreement_
- Must be signed by:
  - The **registered vehicle owner** (seller), AND
  - The **bank's authorised signatory** (bank stamp required)
- **2 sets** of Form 35 are required — both submitted to the RTO.

**✅ YES** → Form 35 (2 sets, both signed) obtained. Proceed to Step B.

**❌ NO — Form 35 not obtained**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Download Form 35 from `parivahan.gov.in` → Forms → Form 35.
> 2. Or collect from any RTO office.
> 3. Take it to your bank. The bank's authorised signatory and the vehicle owner must both sign.
> 4. Ask the bank to do Form 35 simultaneously when they issue the NOC — saves a second trip.
> 5. Make **2 complete signed sets** — both are submitted to the RTO.
> 6. **If bank refuses to sign Form 35 after full repayment:** Raise a formal complaint to the bank's Nodal Officer. The bank is legally obligated to sign Form 35 after full repayment — they cannot withhold it.

---

## STEP B — HP TERMINATION AT THE RTO

---

### L-05 🔴 CRITICAL — HP termination filed at the PARENT / ORIGINAL RTO only

**What to check:**

- HP termination must be done at the **specific RTO where the vehicle was originally registered** — not any other RTO, not a more convenient RTO.
- Example: Vehicle with MH-01 registration → HP termination at MH-01 RTO (Tardeo, Mumbai Central).
- Out-of-state vehicle → HP termination at that state's original RTO.

**✅ YES** → Application filed at the correct RTO. Proceed to L-06.

**❌ NO — Filed at wrong RTO or not yet filed**

> **→ FIX:**
>
> 1. Identify the correct parent RTO from the vehicle's registration number (first 4 characters = state + RTO code).
> 2. Withdraw any application incorrectly filed at a different RTO.
> 3. Re-file at the correct parent RTO.
> 4. For out-of-state vehicles: the seller must physically travel to the original state's RTO — Maharashtra RTO cannot process HP termination for vehicles registered in other states.

---

| Document                          | Notes                                            |

### L-11 🔴 CRITICAL — (If seller defaulted / absconded with RC) Financier's legal remedy known

`[N/A in standard transactions]`

> 1. If the registered owner (seller/borrower) has **refused to deliver the RC** or **absconded** after repossession by the financier:

> 3. Payment cannot be made by cash or online for road tax at most Maharashtra RTOs — DD is required.

## TOP 10 CRITICAL PITFALLS — DO NOT MISS

1. **Buying without VAHAN check** — never skip this; stolen vehicles and vehicles with heavy challans are commonly sold privately.
2. **Accepting DigiLocker RC** — explicitly not valid for Maharashtra RTO transfer; original RC only.
3. **Purchasing with active hypothecation** — you cannot transfer an RC that has an active bank lien; HP termination is mandatory, not optional.
4. **Skipping the notarised sale agreement** — your only legal protection in a dispute.
5. **Missing the 30-day buyer deadline** — ₹500 late fee + ongoing legal liability.
6. **Seller missing the 14-day RTO notification** — seller remains legally liable for all post-sale incidents until acknowledged by the RTO.
7. **Using an expired Bank NOC** — NOC is valid for 90 days only; if expired, request a fresh one.
8. **Filing HP termination at the wrong RTO** — must always be the parent / original RTO where the vehicle is registered.
9. **Ignoring traffic challans** — RTO will block transfer; buyer should verify and seller must clear before sale.
10. **Not obtaining RTO acknowledgement** — seller's receipt / RPAD acknowledgement is the legal indemnification from all post-sale liability.
